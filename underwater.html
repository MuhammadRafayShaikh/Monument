Underwater Wonders

The list of the Seven Underwater Wonders of the World was created by CEDAM International (Conservation, Education, Diving, Awareness and Marine research), a non-profit organization of divers. This organization is dedicated to oceanic research and conservation. CEDAM began the Seven Underwater Wonders project in 1989. At this time, it was decided that a list of sea wonders should be created in order to protect these special locations in the under water world, before they are lost forever. Each of these wonders of the sea was selected because of its beauty, its marine life, its scientific research value and its environmental importance. Finally, the results were revealed at the National Aquarium in Washington DC, and the list includes the following amazing places:

Deep Sea Vents

Also known as hydrothermal vents. They sustain some of the most bizarre and impressive ecosystems of the world, since these ecosystems are to a great depth.

Puerto Ayora, Ecuador

Deep Sea Vents

Deep-sea vents are hydrothermal vents on the ocean floor, emitting hot, mineral-rich fluids that support unique ecosystems thriving in extreme conditions.

History

Discovered in 1977, deep-sea vents are hydrothermal systems on the ocean floor, fostering unique ecosystems fueled by mineral-rich, superheated water.

Galapagos Islands

This archipelago is the home of some of the most beautiful and impressive species of the world, which inspired to Charles Darwin to create his Theory of Evolution.

Galapagos Islands

History

The Galápagos Islands, located in the Pacific Ocean, gained historical significance for inspiring Charles Darwin's theory of evolution due to their unique and diverse ecosystems.

Lake Baikal

Lake Baikal is the world's deepest and oldest freshwater lake, located in Siberia, Russia.

History

Lake Baikal, located in Siberia, is the world's deepest freshwater lake, reaching depths of over 5,387 feet (1,642 meters), and it holds around 20% of the Earth's unfrozen freshwater.
