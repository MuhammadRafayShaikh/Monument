New Seven Wonders

The New 7 Wonders Foundation is a private organization established in 2001, which is dedicated to investing in good causes related with the monument preservation and reconstruction around the world. This organisation prepared a global contest on the internet, whose goal was to find the Seven Wonders of the Modern World. This election was made through a global vote on its website. Finally, on July 07, 2007 the results were announced and these are the proclaimed New Seven Wonders of the World.

Chichen Itza

Chichen Itza, the ancient Mayan citadel, is without doubt the most amazing religions monument in Mexican history.

Yucatan, Mexico

Chichen Itza

Chichen Itza

Chichén Itzá, a marvel of ancient Mayan engineering and cosmological significance, stands as a testament to a sophisticated civilization's architectural prowess and cultural legacy.

History

Chichen Itza, an ancient Mayan city in Mexico, stands as a testament to the advanced civilization with its iconic pyramid, El Castillo, showcasing astronomical precision and cultural significance.

Machu Picchu

The most beautiful Inca city and the main archaeological site of America, it is the most popular tourist destination in Peru.

Cusco, Peru

Machu Pichu

Machu Pichu

Machu Picchu is an ancient Incan citadel perched high in the Andes Mountains, renowned for its breathtaking architecture and panoramic views.

History

Machu Picchu, an ancient Incan citadel in Peru, was built in the 15th century and later abandoned, only to be rediscovered in 1911 by Hiram Bingham.

Christ the Redeemer

On top of Corcovado Mountain overlooking Rio, this 38m high statue waits for visitors from the whole world with open arms.

Rio De Janeiro, Brazil

Christ The Redeemer

Christ The Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer is an iconic colossal statue atop Rio de Janeiro's Corcovado mountain, symbolizing Brazil's Christian heritage and offering breathtaking panoramic views of the city.

History

Christ the Redeemer, an iconic statue overlooking Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, was completed in 1931 and stands atop the Corcovado mountain. Designed by Paul Landowski and constructed by Heitor da Silva Costa, this 30-meter tall symbol of Christianity has become a globally recognized landmark, representing both religious significance and Brazilian cultural pride.

Great Wall of China

One of the most famous landmarks in the world and the only man made structure that can be seen from space.

Beijing China

Great Wall Of China

Great Wall Of China

The Great Wall of China, one of the most iconic landmarks in the world, spans over 13,000 miles across northern China. Built over centuries, it is a testament to ancient Chinese engineering and military prowess, serving as a protective barrier against invasions.

History

Constructed by various Chinese dynasties starting from the 7th century BC, The Great Wall of China aimed to defend against nomadic invasions from the north. Its architectural marvel and historical significance make it a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of China's rich cultural heritage.

Petra

Near Aqaba, Jordan you can follow in the footsteps of Indiana Jones, to discover these awe inspiring giant red mountains and extensive mausoleums.

Aqaba Jordan

Petra

Petra

Petra, located in southern Jordan, is a renowned archaeological site known for its impressive rock-cut architecture and ancient city ruins. Carved into rose-red cliffs, Petra was the capital of the Nabataean Kingdom in the 4th century BCE and later became a major trading hub, featuring iconic structures like Al-Khazneh and the Monastery.

History

Founded around the 6th century BCE, Petra thrived as a vital center for trade along the Silk Road, connecting the Arabian Peninsula with the Mediterranean. Its intricate structures and water management systems showcase the advanced engineering skills of the Nabataeans. Eventually, the city fell into decline after the Roman Empire annexed the region in 106 CE, and it remained forgotten by the Western world until its rediscovery in the early 19th century.

Roman Colosseum

An elliptical amphitheatre, the Coliseum is one of the greatest works of Roman architecture and engineering.

Rome, Italy

Roman Colosseum

Roman Colosseum

The Roman Colosseum, an iconic amphitheater in the heart of Rome, is a symbol of ancient Roman engineering and entertainment. Built in the 1st century CE, it hosted gladiator contests and public spectacles, accommodating tens of thousands of spectators, showcasing the grandeur of Roman architecture and culture.

History

The Roman Colosseum, officially known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, was commissioned by Emperor Vespasian of the Flavian dynasty in AD 72 and completed in AD 80 by his successor Titus. This monumental arena, capable of holding 50,000 to 80,000 spectators, was used for gladiatorial contests, animal hunts, mock sea battles, and public executions. Over the centuries, it faced damage from earthquakes and stone-robbers but remains an enduring symbol of ancient Rome's architectural and cultural prowess.

Taj Mahal

Perhaps the most famous landmark in India, the Taj Mahal is a truly magnificent monument that reflects an immense love.

Agra, India

Taj Mahal

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal: an ivory masterpiece that breathes eternal love and architectural grace along the banks of the Yamuna River in Agra, India.

History

Commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century, the Taj Mahal stands as a testament to undying love, built in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Its construction spanned over two decades, culminating in 1653 as a majestic marvel of Mughal architecture in Agra, India.
