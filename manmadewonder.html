Man Made Wonders

The most famous list of the Man Made Wonders was conceived by the travel writer Howard Hillman, who published over 25 books about travelling, cooking and wine. He also wrote a prestigious column in the travel section of famous “New York Times”.

Initially, his list was personal, but in 1968, when he showed it to some travellers in Machu Picchu (Peru), he decided to realize a consensus list. He compiled this list with the following top man-made tourist travel wonders of the world:

Angkor Wat

It is a wonderful temple in Cambodia constructed by the Khmer dynasty. Get there in time to see the sun rise over the temple for an amazing experience.

Siem Reap, Cambodia

Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat is a UNESCO World Heritage site in Cambodia, renowned for its stunning Khmer architecture and being the largest religious monument globally.

History

Angkor Wat, built in the 12th century, is a UNESCO World Heritage site in Cambodia and the largest religious monument globally, showcasing the pinnacle of Khmer architecture and serving as a Hindu, then Buddhist, temple complex.

Machu Picchu

Machu Pichu

Machu Pichu

Machu Picchu is an ancient Incan citadel perched high in the Andes Mountains, renowned for its breathtaking architecture and panoramic views.

History

Machu Picchu, an ancient Incan citadel in Peru, was built in the 15th century and later abandoned, only to be rediscovered in 1911 by Hiram Bingham.

Karnak Temple

The religious complex of Karnak at Luxor was the most important religious centre in Egypt while Thebes was the capital of the Empire.

Luxor, Egypt

Karnak Temple

Karnak Temple

Karnak Temple, located in Luxor, Egypt, is a vast ancient complex dedicated to various deities and renowned for its impressive Hypostyle Hall with towering columns.

History

The Karnak Temple Complex in Luxor, Egypt, is a vast ancient religious site that evolved over 2,000 years, dedicated to various deities, with construction initiated in the Middle Kingdom around 2000 BCE.

Great Pyramids

The Great Pyramid of Giza , also called Khufu’s Pyramid or the Pyramid of Khufu, and Pyramid of Cheops. The Great Pyramids of Giza, built around 4,500 years ago

Giza, Egypt

Great Pyramids

Great Pyramids

The Great Pyramids of Giza, built around 4,500 years ago, stand as iconic marvels of ancient Egyptian architecture and engineering.

History

The Great Pyramids of Giza, built around 4,500 years ago in ancient Egypt, stand as enduring marvels of engineering and are iconic symbols of the pharaohs' monumental burial tombs.
